## The Vision Behind rosa_qda

Qualitative social research is a core component of social science and humanities studies, as well as practice-based research. However, anyone wishing to analyze qualitative data today (e.g., following Mayring's Qualitative Content Analysis or Grounded Theory) quickly encounters financial barriers: market-leading programs like **MAXQDA** or **Atlas.ti** are proprietary and carry significant licensing costs for students and smaller organizations.

**rosa_qda** aims to fill this gap. Just as **PSPP** represents a free, easily accessible alternative to SPSS in quantitative analysis, rosa_qda aims to offer students and researchers a free qualitative analysis software.

The goal is not to copy the full feature set of commercial giants, but to provide the **essential analysis tools** needed for student and practical research, paired with an outstanding user experience.

---

## Why a New Software?

There are already some notable open-source projects in the QDA field, such as:
- **Qualcoder**: Offers excellent analysis and even AI features, but has a very classic, sometimes technically intimidating GUI.
- **OpenQDA** and **ReQual**: Offer great collaboration capabilities, but are also rather technical in setup or daily usage.

The problem: In fields like **Social Work** or general social sciences, many researchers and students are less tech-savvy. A complicated or outdated interface often prevents the software from being adopted.

### Our Approach
* **Familiar Interface:** A design modeled after familiar structures (e.g., document system, code system, text editor, and coding areas) to make onboarding as smooth as possible.
* **Easy Installation:** No complex database setups. The software should be downloadable and ready to run.
* **Focus on Essentials:** Clear, well-structured tools instead of overloaded menus.

## Who is Behind the Project?

My name is **Jonas Ritscher**. I study Social Work and am currently writing my Master's thesis. Frustrated by the requirement to use expensive, proprietary software during my studies, I decided to launch rosa_qda.

Since I am not a trained computer scientist, this project grows through a lot of personal commitment and modern software prototyping ("Vibecoding"). The goal is to build an open, collaborative project where code, feedback, and ideas from the community are shared.

## How to Contribute

The project is still in its early stages, and any help is welcome! You can get involved in several ways:
1. **Feedback & Testing:** Use the prototypes for your work and report bugs or suggestions via GitHub Issues.
2. **Development:** If you have programming skills in Python (backend) or JavaScript (frontend), we welcome pull requests!
3. **Documentation:** Help write clear guides and tutorials for other students.

Visit us on [GitHub](https://github.com/JonasRitscher/rosa_qda) to see the current development status or contribute directly.
